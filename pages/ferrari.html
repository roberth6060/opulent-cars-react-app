Ferrari

Created: Dec. 9, 2022
By Robert Haye

Ferrari is an Italian luxury sports car manufacturer founded by Enzo Ferrari in 1939. The company's headquarters are located in Maranello, Italy, and it is one of the most well-known and successful sports car brands in the world.
Ferrari was born in 1898 in Modena, Italy. He was a racing driver and team manager for Alfa Romeo before starting his own company, Scuderia Ferrari, in 1929. The company acted as a racing team for Alfa Romeo, but Ferrari's true passion was building sports cars. In 1939, he left Alfa Romeo to start his own car company, which he named Auto Avio Costruzioni.
Ferrari's first car, the Tipo 815, was a race car that was built in 1940. However, World War II interrupted the company's operations, and it was not until 1947 that the first Ferrari-badged car, the 125 S, was produced. This car was powered by a 1.5-liter V12 engine, and it marked the beginning of Ferrari's long and successful history of building high-performance sports cars.
In the 1950s, Ferrari established itself as a dominant force in racing, winning numerous championships and setting numerous speed records. The company's cars were known for their impressive speed and handling, and they quickly gained a reputation for being some of the best sports cars in the world.
In the 1960s, Ferrari continued to innovate and release new models, including the 250 GTO, which became one of the most iconic and valuable sports cars ever produced. The company also expanded its operations, opening new factories and showrooms around the world.
In the decades that followed, Ferrari continued to release new models and improve upon existing ones. Some of the most famous Ferrari models include the 365 GTB/4, also known as the "Daytona," and the Testarossa, which was featured in the popular 1980s television show "Miami Vice."
Today, Ferrari is a globally recognized brand and a symbol of luxury and performance. The company's cars are highly sought after by collectors and enthusiasts, and they continue to be known for their impressive speed and handling.
